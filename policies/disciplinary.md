# Disciplinary Policy

## Section 1: Purpose

This policy sets out how the Infinity Cycling Club investigates and responds
to reports of member misconduct, in a manner that is fair to all parties and
consistent with the Constitution.

## Section 2: Reportable Conduct

The following conduct by a member may be subject to this policy:

2.1 Verbal abuse, harassment, or intimidation of another member, marshal, or
event official.

2.2 Dangerous or reckless riding that endangers other road users.

2.3 Breach of the Code of Conduct during a Club ride, race, or event.

2.4 Bringing the Club into disrepute through conduct connected to Club
activities.

## Section 3: Disciplinary Process

### 3.1 Receiving a Complaint

Any member may submit a complaint in writing to the Secretary. The Secretary
must acknowledge receipt within 5 business days and forward the complaint to
EXCO.

### 3.2 Investigation

EXCO must appoint at least two EXCO members, none of whom have a conflict of
interest under Constitution Section 6.2, to investigate the complaint. The
investigation must give the member complained against a fair opportunity to
respond in writing.

### 3.3 Hearing

Where the investigation finds a reasonable basis for the complaint, EXCO must
convene a disciplinary hearing. The member concerned may attend, be
accompanied by a support person, and respond to the allegations before any
sanction is decided.

### 3.4 Sanctions

Following a hearing, EXCO may impose one or more of the following sanctions,
proportionate to the severity and history of the conduct:

* a verbal warning;
* a written warning placed on the member's file;
* suspension of membership and riding privileges for a period determined by
  EXCO, not exceeding 12 months; or
* termination of membership, in accordance with Membership Policy Section 4.

### 3.5 Appeals

A member may appeal a sanction in writing to EXCO within 14 days. An appeal
must be heard by EXCO members who did not sit on the original hearing panel,
where practicable.

## Section 4: Record Keeping

EXCO must keep a confidential record of all complaints, investigations, and
outcomes for a minimum of 3 years.
