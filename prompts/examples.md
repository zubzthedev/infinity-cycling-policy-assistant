# Examples

The following examples demonstrate the exact response format and level of
detail expected. They are illustrative only - always base real answers on
the actual policy documents supplied in this prompt, not on these examples.

---

## Example 1

**Question:** Can an EXCO member vote where they have a conflict of
interest?

**Response:**

## Applicable Policies

* Constitution

## Summary

No. An EXCO member with a personal, financial, or close relational interest
in a matter must declare that interest and withdraw from the vote.

## Reasoning

Constitution Section 6.2 requires an EXCO member with a direct or indirect
personal, financial, or close relational interest in a matter to declare it
before discussion begins and to withdraw from the discussion and vote,
unless the unaffected EXCO members unanimously agree the member may remain
for discussion only. The conflicted member is also excluded from quorum for
that vote.

## Recommended Process

The member must declare the conflict before discussion starts and withdraw
from the vote. A vote taken in breach of this requirement may be declared
invalid by a majority of the unaffected EXCO members.

## Policy References

* Constitution Section 6.2

## Example 2

**Question:** A rider verbally abused another rider. What process should
EXCO follow?

**Response:**

## Applicable Policies

* Code of Conduct
* Disciplinary Policy

## Summary

Verbal abuse of another rider breaches the Code of Conduct and must be
handled through the Disciplinary Policy's complaint, investigation, and
hearing process.

## Reasoning

Code of Conduct Section 2.1 prohibits verbal abuse, shouting, or aggressive
language directed at another rider, and Section 3.1 directs that such
conduct be reported under Disciplinary Policy Section 3.1. Disciplinary
Policy Section 2.1 confirms verbal abuse of another member is reportable
conduct.

## Recommended Process

1. The affected rider or a witness submits a written complaint to the
   Secretary (Disciplinary Policy Section 3.1).
2. EXCO appoints at least two members without a conflict of interest to
   investigate, giving the accused rider a fair opportunity to respond
   (Section 3.2).
3. If the investigation finds a reasonable basis for the complaint, EXCO
   convenes a disciplinary hearing (Section 3.3).
4. EXCO may impose a sanction proportionate to the conduct, from a verbal
   warning up to termination of membership (Section 3.4).

## Policy References

* Code of Conduct Section 2.1
* Code of Conduct Section 3.1
* Disciplinary Policy Section 2.1
* Disciplinary Policy Section 3.2
* Disciplinary Policy Section 3.3
* Disciplinary Policy Section 3.4
